Matthew Martinez
Orchestra Director · JHS & NWPMS

Mr. Martinez proudly serves as the Orchestra Director for both Jacksonville High School and Northwoods Park Middle School, guiding string students from their first note through advanced honor ensemble performance. A double bassist and music educator originally from Clayton, NC, he has played the double bass for over 15 years and performs extensively throughout North Carolina, Tennessee, and Virginia, including regular appearances with the Johnson City Symphony Orchestra (TN) and past performances with the Asheville Symphony Orchestra (NC). His performance background spans orchestras, historically informed performance (HIP) ensembles, musicals, opera, wind ensembles, and bluegrass groups, and he has tracked movie scores and contemporary music projects at the Robert F. Gilley Recording Studio in Boone, NC.

Mr. Martinez holds a Bachelor of Music in Instrumental Music Education (2018) and a Master of Music in Double Bass Performance (2020), both from Appalachian State University. Under his direction, the JHS and NWPMS orchestra programs consistently earn high ratings at state adjudication events such as MPA and Music in the Parks, as well as in public performances throughout the community.

Beyond the orchestra room, Mr. Martinez co-directs the annual spring musical at Jacksonville High School and has conducted productions including Into the Woods, The Addams Family Musical, and Anastasia the Musical. He also performs as a double bassist and electric bassist in local professional productions. He currently resides in Jacksonville, NC, with his wife, Allison, and their three dogs, Athena, Atlas, and Simba.

About Our Program

JHS Cardinal Orchestra

The Jacksonville High School Orchestra program is committed to providing students with a rigorous and rewarding musical education. As part of one of Onslow County's premier high schools — and North Carolina's only International Baccalaureate high school — our orchestra program reflects that same commitment to excellence.

Students in our program develop technical mastery, musical sensitivity, and the discipline that carries into every area of their lives.

Our Ensembles

  • Concert Orchestra (Grades 9–12)
  • Symphonic Orchestra (Advanced)
  • Chamber Strings
  • String Quartet / Small Ensembles

Source: ncmea.net · NCMEA Orchestra Site

Not yet announced
Location
Not yet announced
Last Cycle (2025–26)
Held in January at Gray's Creek HS, $10–$20 registration
Eligibility
High school students (JHS)
2026–27 Cycle: Audition materials, dates, and registration details for the 2026–27 school year have not yet been released by NCMEA as of this update. NCMEA typically publishes the next cycle's repertoire, scales, and registration details in the fall.

For reference, 2025–26 Audition Materials were:
  • Violin: Bach Concerto No. 1
  • Cello: Vivaldi Sonata No. 1
  • Bass: Vivaldi Sonata No. 5
2025–26 Scale Requirements (Violin): C, G, D, A, B♭ Major (3 octaves); F Major (2 octaves); any melodic minor (3 octaves). Slurred 4 notes per bow; arpeggios 3 notes per bow.

Southeast Honors String Festival

Source: UNCG College of Visual & Performing Arts — hosted by the UNCG School of Music
2025 Festival Dates
October 17–19, 2025 (15th Annual)
Location
UNC Greensboro, Greensboro, NC
Application Deadline
Audition recordings due mid-September (e.g., Sept. 18, 2025)
Cost
$65/student (includes Saturday lunch & dinner)
Eligibility: Open to students in grades 9–12. JHS has been a participating school in past years of this festival.

Format: An intensive three-day festival conducted by Dr. Jungho Kim, Director of Orchestras at UNCG, with guest artist/clinician sessions. Some students may also be selected for a complimentary private lesson with UNCG string faculty based on their audition recording. The festival concludes with a public concert by the Honors String Orchestra in Tew Recital Hall.

2026 dates have not yet been announced by UNCG as of this update — this section will be updated once the next cycle is published.
UNCG String Festival Page ↗

NC-ASTA All-State String Orchestra Festival & Clinic

Source: ncasta.wordpress.com · NC chapter of the American String Teachers Association
2025 Festival Date
Saturday, October 4, 2025 (65th Annual)
Location
Northwest Guilford High School, Greensboro, NC
Application Deadline
Typically mid-September (e.g., Sept. 17, 2025)
Cost
$75/student (includes T-shirt, lunch & participation)
Eligibility: Open to North Carolina's top string students in grades 5–12, sponsored by a current NC-ASTA member director.

Format: A one-day festival featuring full ensemble rehearsals with a guest conductor, sectionals led by professional musicians, and a public performance — selection based on student application, recorded excerpts, and teacher recommendation.

2026 dates have not yet been announced. This section will be updated as soon as NC-ASTA publishes the next cycle's information.

Note: this is distinct from the NCMEA/NC Bandmasters-sponsored "NC All-State Orchestra Clinic," a separate event requiring prior Regional Orchestra or All-State Band participation. NC-ASTA's festival is its own standalone audition pathway.

About Our Program

NWPMS Dolphin Orchestra

The Northwoods Park Middle School Orchestra gives 6th, 7th, and 8th grade students the opportunity to develop real musical skill and artistry. Our Dolphin Orchestra builds the foundation that leads students to JHS and beyond.

As an AVID School community, NWPMS values organization, persistence, and high expectations — the same values that define excellent orchestral musicians.

Our Ensembles

  • Beginning Strings (Grade 6)
  • Intermediate Orchestra (Grades 6–7)
  • Advanced Orchestra (Grades 7–8)
  • Small Chamber Ensembles
